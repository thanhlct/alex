Introduction ▴

Your task is to call an automated phone system for New York public transport information.

  • You must be a NATIVE speaker of English.
  • You must call a US toll-free phone number. If you call from a US land line, the call is completely free.
  • This Job requires you to talk naturally to the system.
  • Once the call is finished, you will be given a four-digit code which will enable a short feedback form.
  • After filling in the feedback form, the Job is successfully done!
  • Your code will only be valid for 15 minutes, so be sure to fill in the form right after you finish the call.

All data is collected anonymously by the Institute of Formal and Applied Linguistics, Charles University in Prague for research, development, and commercial deployment of spoken dialogue systems and similar applications involving speech.

Before you participate in this job for the first time, please take a moment to look at the instructions, example of a dialogue and the statement of consent. By participating in this Job, you agree with the terms and conditions stated in the consent form.

STATEMENT OF CONSENT

You have volunteered to participate in a study conducted at the Institute of Formal and Applied Linguistics at the Charles University in Prague.

This study is concerned with how people interact with spoken dialogue systems. In the course of this study, you will be interacting with a dialogue system. All interactions with the dialogue system will be recorded for further study and commercial deployment of spoken dialogue systems and similar applications involving speech. After completing the task(s), you will be asked several questions about your experience. There are no known expected physical discomforts or risks involved in your participation.

For the purposes of the Czech Republic 101/2000 Data Protection Act, you give your permission for audio and video recordings, transcriptions, and non-identifying information (e.g. gender, age, mother tongue) to be electronically stored, utilised and shared for purposes of research, development, and commercial deployment of spoken dialogue systems and similar applications involving speech. No identifying information gathered in this study will be disclosed to any persons other than the researchers and their collaborators.

Results of this study will appear in academic journals in terms of group data. If any individual data will be presented, the data will be totally anonymous, without any means of identifying the individuals involved. The researchers will be glad to answer any questions about this study. However, answers that could influence the outcome of the study will be deferred until you have completed the study.

Every effort is made to conduct this study in compliance with the appropriate professional guidelines.
