The audio tracks you will be transcribing are a part of a telephone conversation. The telephone conversation takes place in New York and the caller is looking for information on public transport.

Please, try to transcribe as good as possible. If there is a faulty transcription found, you will not be able to collect your reward. Please listen carefully to avoid misheard words, and read your transcription before you send to avoid typos. The test questions are very strict and allow neither typos nor misheard words.

Spelling

Write the transcriptions in LOWER CASE without punctuation, which means NO COMMAS! (apostrophe allowed) and please, write WORDS FOR NUMBERS.  Only the characters a-z and ' ( ) are allowed (lowercase, apostrophe and parenthesis).

Examples:

  • "goodbye", not "Good bye" and not "good-bye"
  • "you're", not "You're", not "youre" and not "you are" (unless are is explicitly pronounced of course)
  • "six p m", not "6 PM" and not "six pm"
  • "hi how are you", not "hi, how are you" and not "Hi how are you"
  • "when will i arrive", not "when will I arrive" and not "When will I arrive"

Incomplete Words

If a word is pronounced incomplete, put "-" (dash) before or after the pronounced part of the word.

Examples: "incomp-" "-eech" "-crastinat-"

Non-speech Sounds

For non-speech sounds and unintelligible tracks, please use the following symbols (including the parentheses):

symbol meaning
(breath) the sound of breathing, sniffing etc.
(cough) the sound of coughing
(hum) humming (all of hum, erm, ehm hmm etc.)
(laugh) laughter
(noise) noise on the background
(sil) silence, if it is prominent or if there is nothing else to transcribe
(unint) unintelligible speech
